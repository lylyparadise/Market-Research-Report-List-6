<p><p>The body composition meter market encompasses two primary types: digital and electric devices. Digital meters use advanced sensors and software to provide precise data on body composition, including metrics like fat percentage, muscle mass, and hydration levels. They offer user-friendly interfaces and often connect to apps for tracking progress. Electric meters, on the other hand, typically rely on bioelectrical impedance analysis, sending a small electrical current through the body to measure resistance and estimate body composition. Both types cater to health-conscious consumers seeking detailed insights into their physical well-being.</p></p>
