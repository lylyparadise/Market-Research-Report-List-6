<p><p>Polyoxymethylene (POM) is a high-performance engineering thermoplastic known for its rigidity, durability, and low friction properties. The market is primarily segmented into two types: POM-H and POM-C. POM-H, or homopolymer, offers superior mechanical properties and is often used in applications requiring high stiffness and strength. POM-C, or copolymer, provides enhanced chemical resistance and lower temperature sensitivity, making it suitable for more varied applications. Both types find extensive use in automotive, electronics, and consumer goods industries.</p></p>
