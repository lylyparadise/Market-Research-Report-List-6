<p><p>The recycled polyester fiber market is primarily divided into two types: recycled polyester filament and recycled polyester staple fiber. Recycled polyester filament is produced in continuous strands, often used for making fabrics that require strength and durability, like upholstery or technical textiles. In contrast, recycled polyester staple fiber consists of shorter fibers, suitable for applications like insulation, filling materials, or blended textiles. Both types contribute to sustainability efforts by repurposing plastic waste, reducing reliance on virgin polyester materials.</p></p>
