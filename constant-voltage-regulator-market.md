<p><p>The constant voltage regulator market encompasses two primary types: AC voltage stabilizers and DC voltage stabilizers. AC voltage stabilizers are designed to maintain a steady voltage output for alternating current systems, protecting sensitive electronic equipment from voltage fluctuations. In contrast, DC voltage stabilizers regulate direct current output, ensuring consistent voltage levels for devices that require a stable power supply. Both types are essential in various applications, including industrial, commercial, and residential settings, to enhance the reliability and performance of electrical devices.</p></p>
