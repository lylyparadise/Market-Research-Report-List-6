<p><p>Dry, condensed, and evaporated milk are forms of milk that have undergone processes to extend shelf life and alter their texture and flavor. Dry milk, or powdered milk, is created by removing moisture from liquid milk, resulting in a shelf-stable product ideal for baking and cooking. Condensed milk involves the removal of water and the addition of sugar, yielding a sweet and thick product commonly used in desserts. Evaporated milk is produced by heating milk to remove about 60% of its water content, providing a creamy texture without added sugar, making it versatile for both sweet and savory dishes.</p><p>The Dry, Condensed and Evaporated Milk Market is expected to grow at a CAGR of 6% during the forecast period. Key factors driving this growth include the increasing demand for convenience foods, the rising trend of home baking, and the expanding use of these products in processed and ready-to-eat meals. Additionally, the growing preference for shelf-stable products among consumers, coupled with advancements in packaging and preservation techniques, is contributing to the market's upward trajectory. Overall, the market is poised for steady growth, reflecting broader trends in consumer preferences towards versatile and convenient food solutions.</p></p>
