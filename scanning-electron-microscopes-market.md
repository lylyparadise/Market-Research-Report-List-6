<p><p>The Scanning Electron Microscopes (SEM) market consists of several types, each serving unique applications. Conventional SEM operates under high vacuum, providing detailed surface images. Variable Pressure or Low Vacuum SEM allows imaging of wet or non-conductive samples without extensive preparation. Cryo-SEM preserves biological samples by freezing them, while Environmental SEM enables observation in varying gaseous environments, accommodating more complex samples. Other markets may include specialized SEM variations or hybrid systems, catering to niche research or industrial needs.</p></p>
