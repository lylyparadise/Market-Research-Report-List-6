<p><p>Opioid Use Disorder (OUD) treatments comprise distinct market types: Buprenorphine, Methadone, and Naltrexone. Buprenorphine, a partial opioid agonist, reduces cravings and withdrawal symptoms while having a lower risk of misuse. Methadone, a full agonist, is effective in maintenance therapy but requires careful monitoring due to its potential for abuse. Naltrexone, an opioid antagonist, blocks opioid effects and is typically used after detoxification. Each treatment option addresses OUD from different angles, catering to diverse patient needs and treatment approaches.</p></p>
