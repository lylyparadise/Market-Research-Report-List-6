<p><p>Threading tools are categorized into external and internal markets based on the application of the threading process. The external market involves tools designed for creating threads on the outer surfaces of components, such as bolts and screws, typically utilizing taps and dies. Conversely, the internal market focuses on tools for generating threads within cylindrical or hollow objects, utilizing internal taps and threading machines. Each market serves specific industrial needs, emphasizing the importance of precision and efficiency in various manufacturing processes.</p></p>
