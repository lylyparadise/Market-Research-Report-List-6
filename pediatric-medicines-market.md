<p><p>The pediatric medicines market can be categorized into enteral, parenteral, and topical types. Enteral medications are administered through the gastrointestinal tract, often in liquid form, suitable for infants and children. Parenteral medications are delivered via injections or infusions, providing rapid effects for critically ill children or those unable to take oral medications. Topical medications are applied directly to the skin for localized treatment, commonly used for conditions like rashes or infections. Each type addresses specific pediatric healthcare needs.</p></p>
