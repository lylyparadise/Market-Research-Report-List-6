<p><p>The laboratory rheometers market comprises several types, each serving distinct testing needs. Dynamic rheometers assess material behavior under varying conditions, measuring viscosity and elasticity. Capillary rheometers evaluate flow properties by forcing materials through narrow orifices, ideal for high-viscosity fluids. Torque rheometers focus on measuring the torque required to mix or process materials, suitable for evaluating formulations. Other types include specialized devices for niche applications, contributing to a comprehensive understanding of material properties in research and development across various industries.</p></p>
